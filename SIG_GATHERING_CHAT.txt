The First Legal Question: Can You Pay Per Signature?

This varies by state.

There are generally three categories:

1. Explicitly allowed

Some states allow paying circulators by signature.

2. Allowed with restrictions

Some states prohibit payment based solely on the number of signatures but allow hourly wages, bonuses, etc.

3. Explicitly prohibited

Some states ban per-signature compensation because of fraud concerns.

California, for example, has historically had litigation around this issue for initiative petitions. Other states have very different rules.

For a nationwide presidential ballot access operation, CampaignOS would probably need a state-by-state compensation engine that knows:

State	Per Signature	Hourly	Hybrid	Notes
State A	✅	✅	✅	
State B	❌	✅	⚠️	
State C	⚠️	✅	✅	

This is something we'd need to research carefully.

L2 and TargetSmart Pricing

Neither publishes full pricing publicly.

Based on campaign industry norms:

Local campaign: low thousands of dollars
Congressional campaign: several thousand to tens of thousands
Statewide campaign: tens of thousands+
Presidential/national access: potentially six figures+ annually

Some L2 integrations advertise roughly $0.25 per voter record with phone/commercial data and $0.07 per verified email, but large customers almost always negotiate custom contracts.

Most enterprise vendors operate on cycle-based or annual licenses rather than simple per-record pricing.

For a serious 2028 effort, a national voter data license would likely be affordable relative to campaign costs.

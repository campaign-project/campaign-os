# Voter-File Acquisition Map (validation-engine prerequisite)

Per-state map of how to obtain the statewide voter registration file — the data the validation
engine matches petition signatures against. **51 of 51 mapped.** Mapping only: the team
pulls files via the paths below (we don't bulk-download multi-GB PII here).

> **Verified as of 2026-06-06.** Fees and access rules drift (e.g. AL HB67 cut its statewide fee
> ~\$38k→\$1k effective 2026-06-01); re-verify before relying on a figure. Run the freshness check:
> `cd prototypes/validation-engine && npm run freshness` (flags records past their 180-day re-check window).

> **Compliance:** every state limits the file to **political / election / petition use** (commercial use
> barred); several require a **sworn affidavit / penalty-of-perjury** certification, and misuse is a crime
> (e.g. CA Elec. Code 18109, IN Title 3 Art. 14). **Petition signature verification is a permitted use** —
> honor the use restriction + certification.

## Per-state detail

### Alabama — 🟠 paid
- **cost:** $1,000 cap statewide (HB67, eff. 2026-06-01); legacy rate $0.01/name, $1.00 min (~$38,000 statewide pre-cap)
- **who may obtain:** General public — "Anyone can request the voter list through a form on the secretary of state's website." Statute (§17-4-38) directs the SoS to "provide access to lists and voter history information ... to anyone making application." No candidate/committee or political-use eligibility gate. Typical buyers are political parties, candidates, consultants, and voter-integrity groups. (Legislators get their district free; courts get a statewide copy for jury lists; other states can get a reciprocal copy once a year.)
- **use restrictions:** HB67 (eff. June 1, 2026) prohibits commercial use of the voter list and bars the SoS from releasing voters' phone numbers, driver license numbers, voter ID numbers, email addresses, and month/day of birth (SSNs and emails were already withheld). Purchase certification states all sales are final and the file expires 7 days after purchase. (Specific penalty language not captured from the statute text; PDF was non-extractable.)
- **process:** Two routes: (1) Instant online purchase + download via the SoS portal at alabamainteractive.org (operated by Alabama Interactive, LLC) — buyer selects geography (city, county, district, or entire state), pays, and has 7 days to download; (2) a mailed/paper "Voter Registration Information Request" form to the Elections Division for city/municipal-district requests. No notarized affidavit or approval step is described for the standard purchase — the requester self-certifies the purchase terms online.
- **fields (name+address+status):** yes
- **verified as of:** 2026-06-06
- **url:** https://www.alabamainteractive.org/sos/voter/voterWelcome.action
- **note:** Alabama sells the statewide voter file to anyone via an instant online portal; HB67, effective June 1, 2026 (days before this research date), caps the price at $1,000 (down from ~$38,000 at the old $0.01/name rate) and prohibits commercial use and release of phone numbers. The file includes name, address, race, gender, date of birth, and 10-election voting history — sufficient to match petition signers to registered voters.

### Alaska — 🟡 free / low-cost
- **cost:** ~$20 for the statewide voter list (price estimate per the U.S. EAC compilation; the Alaska Division of Elections does not publish a per-record fee schedule online and quotes/processes orders through a divisional office). Treat $20 as an estimate, not a guaranteed published figure.
- **who may obtain:** General public — Alaska voter registration records are an "Open" file. Names, addresses, and party affiliation are public and available to anyone who purchases the list (candidates, parties, committees, and the public alike). A voter may individually elect to keep their residential address confidential by providing a separate mailing address (AS 15.07.195(b)).
- **use restrictions:** No statutory eligibility gate, but per the EAC compilation, "even the least restrictive states prohibit the commercial use of voter file information." The file excludes confidential PII (DOB/age, SSN, DL number, voter ID number, place of birth, signature) under AS 15.07.195(a). Specific penalty/affidavit language for commercial-use prohibition was not located on the primary Division of Elections page during this research.
- **process:** Written/in-person request to a Division of Elections divisional office — NOT an instant online download. The Division's Research page states lists are "available for purchase" and to "contact any divisional office for assistance." No public self-serve download portal for the bulk statewide file was found.
- **fields (name+address+status):** yes
- **verified as of:** 2026-06-06
- **url:** https://www.elections.alaska.gov/research/
- **note:** Alaska's statewide voter list is an "Open" public record at a low cost (~$20 per the U.S. EAC), obtained by request to a Division of Elections divisional office rather than instant download. The standard file includes voter name, residential address, and party affiliation — sufficient to match petition signers to registered voters — while DOB, SSN, DL number, voter ID number, place of birth, and signature are statutorily confidential (AS 15.07.195).

### Arizona — 🔴 restricted (gated)
- **cost:** Statutory tiered formula (ARS 16-168(E)): 1–124,999 records = $93.75 + $0.0005/record; 125,000–249,999 = $156.25 + $0.000375/record; 250,000–499,999 = $203.13 + $0.00025/record; 500,000–999,999 = $265.63 + $0.000125/record; 1,000,000+ records = $328.13 + $0.0000625/record. A full statewide file (~4.3M registered voters) falls in the top tier: ~$328.13 + $0.0000625/record (on the order of a few hundred dollars total).
- **who may obtain:** Not the general public for any purpose. Free electronic copies go to county/state party chairmen of qualifying parties. Anyone else (candidates, registered political committees, etc.) may purchase copies only for a statutorily "authorized use" — i.e. political/political-party activity, a political campaign, an election, redistricting, signature verification on petitions/candidate filings, or another use specifically authorized by law. Commercial use is prohibited.
- **use restrictions:** Political-use-only: data "may be used only for purposes relating to a political or political party activity, a political campaign or an election... and may not be used for a commercial purpose." Signature verification on petitions and candidate filings is expressly authorized. Reselling/transferring for an unauthorized purpose or posting it on the internet is prohibited. A violation of the use restriction or resale/distribution restriction "is guilty of a class 6 felony." Sensitive fields (full DOB, SSN, driver license number, voter signature, email) are excluded from general reproduction but may be used for petition signature verification.
- **process:** Written request for an authorized use submitted to the county recorder (or the Secretary of State / officer in charge of elections); the office prepares additional copies and furnishes them within 30 days of receipt of the request upon payment of the statutory fee. (Verifying petition signatures is an explicitly permitted use, so a campaign/committee qualifies.)
- **fields (name+address+status):** yes
- **verified as of:** 2026-06-06
- **note:** Arizona does not offer a free public statewide voter file download; the file is eligibility- and use-gated (political/campaign/election use only, commercial use is a class 6 felony) and sold on a written request at a statutory tiered fee under ARS 16-168. The standard precinct-list/voter file includes full name, residence and mailing address, party, registration date, birth year and voting history — sufficient to match petition signers to registered voters — and signature verification on petitions is an explicitly authorized use, so a campaign/committee can obtain it. (Official azsos.gov voter-data request page was behind a browser challenge and could not be fetched; statute ARS 16-168 is quoted as the controlling primary source for price, eligibility, fields and penalties.)

### Arkansas — 🟡 free / low-cost
- **cost:** Up to $50 per county on electronic media (≤5,000 voters $10; 5,001–25,000 $25; >25,000 $50); printed lists ≤2¢/name. No statewide SoS file — assembled county-by-county across 75 county clerks (Ark. Code 7-5-109).
- **who may obtain:** General public. Ark. Code 7-5-109 says the county clerk "shall provide the list" upon request and imposes no eligibility limit (not restricted to candidates, committees, parties, or political use). The list is a public record subject only to redaction of "secure voter" addresses.
- **use restrictions:** The statute imposes no commercial-use ban or political-use-only limit on the registered voter list itself. The one carve-out is "secure voters": county clerks "shall not reproduce any address of a secure voter in any format unless otherwise authorized by law," and all furnished lists exclude secure-voter addresses (Ark. Code 7-5-109(a)(2), (c)). No penalty for ordinary use is specified in this section.
- **process:** Written / in-person request to the county clerk in each county (the statewide list is assembled county-by-county; the statewide voter registration database is maintained centrally but the statutory access mechanism is the county clerk). No notarized affidavit or sworn statement of permitted use is required by statute. Delivered on compact disc or other electronic medium, or as a printed list.
- **fields (name+address+status):** yes
- **verified as of:** 2026-06-06
- **url:** https://codes.findlaw.com/ar/title-7-elections/ar-code-sect-7-5-109/
- **note:** Arkansas voter registration lists are obtained from county clerks under Ark. Code 7-5-109 at low statutory fee caps (max $50 per county on electronic media), and the list includes "at least the names, addresses, and precinct numbers of the voters" - sufficient to match petition signers to registered voters. There is no free instant statewide download from the Secretary of State; a true statewide file means requesting from each of the 75 county clerks (the SOS maintains the central voter-registration database but the statutory list-access mechanism is the county clerk).

### California — 🔴 restricted (gated)
- **cost:** $100 flat application fee for the statewide voter registration information file (one price for the whole state, regardless of record count). Single-voter records (≤10) are $30.00/record up to a $100 max. County-specific files vary by county.
- **who may obtain:** Eligibility-gated, NOT general public. Per the SoS: "any candidate for federal, state, or local office, any committee for or against any initiative or referendum measure for which legal publication is made, any person for election, scholarly, journalistic, or political purposes, and any person for governmental purposes, as determined by the Secretary of State." A petition-signature-verification campaign/committee qualifies under the election/political-purpose and candidate/committee categories.
- **use restrictions:** Strong. Use is limited to permitted (election/political/scholarly/journalistic/governmental) purposes only; commercial use is prohibited. Misuse is a crime: Elections Code 18109(a) makes it "a misdemeanor for a person in possession of information ... obtained pursuant to Article 5 ... knowingly to use or permit the use of all or any part of that information for any purpose other than as permitted by law," and 18109(b) makes obtaining/using the data without first complying with the application requirements of 2188 a misdemeanor. The application must state intended use and is certified "under penalty of perjury." Confidential-voter (Safe at Home, etc.) records are excluded entirely.
- **process:** Application form + approval (not an instant download). Submit the "California Voter Registration File Request" application (PDF) to the SoS. Elections Code 2188 requires a signed application stating the requestor's name, residence/business address, ID number, the specific information requested, and a statement of intended use, certified "under penalty of perjury." SoS reviews to confirm the use is legally permitted: "requests can take 7-14 business days to review and process." No refunds once sent.
- **fields (name+address+status):** yes
- **verified as of:** 2026-06-06
- **url:** https://www.sos.ca.gov/elections/voter-registration/voter-registration-information-file-request
- **note:** California does NOT offer a free public download of the statewide voter file; access is eligibility-gated to candidates, committees, and election/political/scholarly/journalistic/governmental users, requiring a sworn application and SoS approval. The statewide file is a low flat $100 fee and the standard Voter File includes name, residential and mailing address, registration date, party preference, and precinct (which carries registration status, including inactive voters on request) — sufficient to match petition signers to registered voters. Misuse is a misdemeanor (Elec. Code 18109).

### Colorado — 🟡 free / low-cost
- **cost:** $50.00 flat for the Statewide Registered Voter List (EX-003), delivered on data disc (per report). Alternatively, a full Election Cycle Subscription Service (weekly-updated EX-003 plus other extracts for the entire statewide election cycle) is $1,000.00. Free public online lookups exist only for individual voters, not the bulk file.
- **who may obtain:** General public — no eligibility gate. Colorado voter registration records are statutorily "public records subject to examination by any person." The Elections Data Request Form asks only for requestor name/company and contact info; it is not limited to candidates, committees, or parties.
- **use restrictions:** Social Security numbers, driver's license/state ID numbers, the SOS-assigned unique voter ID, and electronic copies of voter signatures are statutorily excluded from any release (C.R.S. 1-2-302(8)); preregistrant (under-18) information is kept confidential. Colorado law (C.R.S. 1-2-227 and related statutes) restricts use of voter registration data to election/political purposes and prohibits using it for commercial solicitation; the released file does not contain SSN or signature data, so it supports name/address matching but not signature-image comparison.
- **process:** Application form by mail. Complete the Colorado Department of State "Elections Data Request Form" (PDF), check the box for "Statewide Registered Voter List (EX-003)," enclose a check or money order payable to the Colorado Secretary of State, and mail it to the SOS Elections Division (1700 Broadway, Suite 550, Denver, CO 80290). Requests are returned via first-class mail within 72 hours; for expedited carriers the requestor must include a pre-paid mailer. Subscription service provides electronic download access.
- **fields (name+address+status):** yes
- **verified as of:** 2026-06-06
- **url:** https://www.coloradosos.gov/pubs/elections/forms/dataRequests.pdf
- **note:** A campaign obtains Colorado's statewide voter file via the SOS Elections Data Request Form: the Statewide Registered Voter List (EX-003) costs $50 flat on disc, or $1,000 for the weekly-updated full-cycle subscription.
